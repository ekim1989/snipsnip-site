Health scorecard ?How scoring works: Each metric is compared to its target. Green = at or above target. Amber = within 60% of target (needs attention). Red = below 60% (broken, fix now). Gray = not enough data yet. Targets are based on industry benchmarks for Chrome extension utility tools.

WAU ?Weekly Active Users. How many unique users opened SnipSnip in the last 7 days. This is your best pulse check. If WAU is growing week over week, the product is healthy. If it's flat or declining while total users grows, people are signing up but not sticking around.

MAU ?Monthly Active Users. Unique users in the last 30 days. This is the number investors ask about. For a utility tool, MAU should be 60-80% of total users. If it's below 50%, too many people signed up and forgot about you.

Stickiness ?DAU / MAU ratio. Of your monthly users, what percentage use SnipSnip on any given day? For daily-habit apps (Slack, social media), 25-50% is normal. For occasional utility tools like SnipSnip, 15%+ is healthy. People don't screenshot things every day, so don't compare yourself to messaging apps. Below 10% means people forget about you. These targets are higher than mobile apps because Chrome extensions persist in the toolbar without needing to be re-downloaded. Retention benchmarks are independent of each other.

You need to push every install yourself. Above 1.0 means each user brings in more than one new user, and the product grows on its own. At early stage, 0.1-0.3 is normal. Campaign calculator ?Should I spend money on this campaign? Enter how many people you expect to reach and how much it costs. The calculator projects how many Pro users and MRR you'll get, then tells you if it's worth the spend.

How to read the ROI:
12-month revenue = your new MRR x 12 months
12-month profit = revenue minus your spend
Return multiple = revenue divided by spend (2.0x means you doubled your money)
Breakeven = months until the MRR pays back the spend

Rule of thumb: Breakeven under 3 months = strong yes. 3-6 months = probably worth it. Over 6 months = risky. Over 12 months = don't do it.

Estimated market value ?The minimum you should accept if someone offers to buy SnipSnip. Based on how micro-SaaS Chrome extensions actually sell on Acquire.com and Flippa.

Formula: ARR (MRR x 12) multiplied by a standard multiple based on your revenue bracket:

Pre-revenue: $5,000 floor (code + infrastructure + CWS listing)
Under $12k ARR: 2.5x ARR
$12k-$60k ARR: 3.5x ARR
$60k+ ARR: 4.5x ARR

A strategic buyer (competitor, company wanting your users) would typically pay 2-5x above this number. This is the cash flow floor, not the ceiling.
